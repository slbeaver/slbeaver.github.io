The National Computational Thinking Challenge is the first of its kind in Sri Lanka, which mimics the ‘International Challenge on Informatics and Computational Thinking’ carried out by the International Beaver community.

National Computational Thinking Challenge is an hour long online question paper, where school students of all ages from around the country take part using computers available at schools or at theeir homes with the internet facility.

Take a look at the ‘Sample Questions’ to know how they look like.

If you are a school student, have a chance to put your thinking ability to the test and be awarded with prestigious prizes.


Await for the contest registration to open.

About SL Beaver


SL Beaver is a non profit organization, endorsed by the Ministry of Education, the ICT Agency of Sri Lanka, and the British Computer Society, to organize the National Computational Thinking Challenge for the benefit of Sri Lankan school children.

Sri Lanka has shown a downfall in progress with the International Olympiad in Informatics contest since year 2007.

SL Beaver organization sees the unawareness among students with potential thinking power about this field has caused such an event. The National Computational Thinking Challenge focuses on finding such students, making awareness among them, and possibly guiding them to explore their hidden talents regarding the field.

Divisions


The challenge is available under 3 divisions this year:

Under 15
born after 1st of January 2002
Under 17
born between 1st of January 2000 and 1st of January 2002
Under 19
born between 1st of January 1998 and 1st of January 2000

It doesn't matter if you are a language student or a born mathematician!

The questions only require some basic reasoning ability.
